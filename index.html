Projects

  • Robot Gripper

    SolidWorks

    Designed a robotic gripper for A1, Aliengo, and Go1 robot dogs. Furnished with an A1 and servo motor, the arm has 1 degree of motion and will be featured in an ICRA research paper.

    Wheelchair Stabilizer Project

    Teensy 4.1, Arduino, Solidworks, Matlab

    Sudden acceleration, uneven weight distribution, and uneven surfaces can cause wheelchair users to fallout and suffer injuries. In the event of an imminent fall, the stabilizer quickly corrects the wheelchair position.

    Iris (Low Level Control Board)

    KiCad, SolidWorks

    Designed for communication between the onboard computer and motors in HECTOR, the Dynamic Robotics and Control Lab humanoid robot. Equipped with a Teensy 4.1 MCU, 3 CAN transceivers, 5 RS485 transceivers, and an IMU, Iris is analogous to the cerebellum, enabling motor control (pun intended) throughout HECTOR.

    Talaria

    SolidWorks, 3D Printing, Piezoelectric Technology

    Inspired by the winged shoes of Hermes, I set out with my USC Makers team to design light-up shoes powered by footsteps. After embedding piezoelectric crystals into 3D printed shoe soles, we used a bridge rectifier circuit to supply DC current to LEDs. We successfully powered 8 LEDs per shoe and presented our work at the USC Makers showcase.
